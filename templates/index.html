ABOUT FIRE EYE

The dry period from the end of April until the advent of the monsoon in July is "fire season" in Pakistan’s forests, which cover the Hindu Kush Himalayas in the country’s north. In these days, a tiny spark can turn into an out-of-control wildfire raging across forested regions of Punjab, Kashmir, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, and Balochistan for days. Besides causing a number of casualties, the fires killed trees, which consume centuries-old trees, destroy precious habitats, and kill wildlife. They could also have exacerbated recent flash flooding in some regions. The destruction of medicinal plants, beehives, and the nut-bearing Chilgoza pine, in particular, affects local livelihoods to a great extent. Fire Eye is now here to keep an eye on those flames in check. It detects even trace amounts of fire gases and sounds the alarm before the fire can spread. This buys firefighters valuable time so they can respond that much sooner to put out the blaze.

TREE COVER LOSS DUE TO FIRE IN PAKISTAN

Pakistan has 4.2 million ha of forest and planted trees, which equates to 4.8 percent of the total land area. From 2001 to 2021, Pakistan lost 5.46kha of tree cover from fires and 4.29kha from all other causes of loss. The year with the most tree cover loss due to fires was 2006 with 853ha lost to fires — 63% of all tree cover loss for that year. Among regions of Pakistan, F.A.T.A. had the highest rate of tree cover loss due to fires with an average of 189ha lost per year between 2001 to 2021 year period.
